ABOUT MY PROJECT

Design and Characterization of Ag/SmFeO₃ Perovskite Nanostructures with Improved Electrochemical Properties

About the Project Submitted for B.Sc. Physics at Dr. N.G.P. Arts and Science College, Coimbatore Guided by Dr. K. Girija and co-guided by Dr. R. Ravikumar

Objective

    To synthesize and characterize Ag/SmFeO₃ perovskite nanostructures
    To investigate improved electrochemical properties for sensing and energy applications

Synthesis

    Method used: Hydrothermal Synthesis
    Temperature: 180°C for 12 hours in a Teflon-lined autoclave
    Urea used as surfactant to control particle size and morphology

Characterization Results

    XRD confirmed high crystallinity with average crystallite size of ~54 nm
    SEM revealed rectangular nanostructures of approximately ~500 nm
    Cyclic Voltammetry (CV) showed enhanced current response in modified electrodes
    EIS showed charge transfer resistance reduced from 824.6 Ω → 366.2 Ω

Conclusion

    Ag improves electrical conductivity
    SmFeO₃ provides excellent catalytic activity
    Combined effect makes Ag/SmFeO₃ a strong candidate for electrochemical sensors and energy applications
